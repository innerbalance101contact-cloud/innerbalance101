The Science

Why this works. And why logic alone doesn’t.

The Practices in this library are built on established research in neuroscience, somatic psychology, and nervous system regulation. This is not wellness culture. It is applied science.

Most high achievers already go to the gym. They drink water. They think carefully about what they eat. They understand that the body requires consistent, structured care to perform.

The mind is no different. These Practices are that structure. Not a remedy for a bad week. A discipline for the long game.

You just have to show up.

Dr Stephen Porges’ polyvagal theory established that the autonomic nervous system governs our capacity for emotional regulation, connection, and sound judgment. When it is dysregulated, the prefrontal cortex (the part of the brain responsible for executive function) becomes inaccessible. Rational effort alone cannot override this. The nervous system must be addressed at the body level first.

Porges, S.W. (2011). The Polyvagal Theory. W.W. Norton.   Dana, D. (2018). The Polyvagal Theory in Therapy. W.W. Norton.

Research by Dr Bessel van der Kolk and Peter Levine demonstrates that unprocessed emotional experiences are held in the body as physical tension, posture, and autonomic patterns. Purely cognitive interventions cannot access this layer. Somatic movement, breathwork, and body-based practices activate the vagus nerve and facilitate genuine discharge rather than suppression.

van der Kolk, B. (2014). The Body Keeps the Score. Viking.   Levine, P. (1997). Waking the Tiger. North Atlantic Books.

Multiple randomised controlled trials have found that Emotional Freedom Techniques (EFT) tapping significantly reduces cortisol levels, anxiety, and psychological distress. A 2012 study published in the Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease showed a 24% cortisol reduction in EFT participants versus controls. The mechanism involves stimulating meridian endpoints that send calming signals to the amygdala, interrupting the stress response at its source.

Church, D. et al. (2012). Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease, 200(10).   Clond, M. (2016). Journal of Nervous and Mental Disease, 204(5).

Breathing at approximately six breaths per minute has been shown to increase heart rate variability (HRV), the leading physiological marker of stress resilience and emotional regulation capacity. This directly activates the parasympathetic nervous system, shifting the body from a threat state to a grounded, regulated one. The effect is measurable, repeatable, and begins within 90 seconds.

Lehrer, P. & Gevirtz, R. (2014). Frontiers in Psychology.   Zaccaro, A. et al. (2018). Frontiers in Human Neuroscience.

The Founder

Naomi Lucia knows what it costs to climb. She has built businesses, led teams, made high-stakes decisions under pressure, and pushed through things that looked impossible from the outside.

She also did all of it while silently living through extreme stress, social anxiety, panic attacks, and seasons where she quietly questioned whether any of it was worth it. She showed up. Hit the targets. Rushed out the door to be as productive as expected. And asked herself, most days: is this really what life is supposed to feel like?

She tried everything: therapy, books, yoga, meditation, EFT, Qigong. Each one helped, for a while. But she kept ending up back in the same place: overloaded, reactive, strapped into the same emotional rollercoaster, holding on. The tools were real. The missing piece was integration. No one had taught her how to take what she was learning and actually live it, on ordinary days, inside a real life with real pressure.

That gap is what she built the Practices to close.

She holds an MBA and has spent years in the formal study of mindfulness and emotional regulation, developing and refining this library until the Practices were precise enough to work on a Tuesday morning before a board meeting. She didn't lose her ambition. She stopped abandoning herself to feed it.

She uses them daily. Not as emergency tools. As discipline.

She is not the brand. But she is proof the work is real.
