How to use this chart

Your body speaks before your mind does.
This chart helps you listen.

01
Notice the sensation first

Something feels off — but you can't name it yet. Start with what you notice in the body. Tight chest. Jaw clenched. Heart racing. The sensation is the signal.

02
Find your sensation in the chart

Scan the right-hand column of each category. Match what you feel physically to a sensation listed. This tells you which emotional family you're in.

03
Name the specific emotion

Once you've found the sensation, move left to the emotion name. "Anxious" and "scared" both race your heart — but they need different things. Precision matters.

04
Stay with it — don't fix it

Naming an emotion is not the same as resolving it. Sit with the name for a moment. Research shows that labelling an emotional experience measurably reduces its intensity on its own.

Most people skip straight to thinking about how they feel and miss the feeling entirely. This chart teaches you to reverse that sequence. Body first. Name second. Then — and only then — does the work begin.
